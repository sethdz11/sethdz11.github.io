Cogniscence.AI

At its core, this project explores my hypothesis that the human brain remains the most energy efficient computing architecture in existence. By abstracting and implementing fundamental human cognitive processes into largescale LLM architectures, we are significantly reducing computational resource requirements. This approach serves a dual-purpose: Architectural Efficiency: By mirroring the efficiency of biological neural circuits, we optimize LLM inference and training, drastically cutting down the resource overhead typically required for large-scale models. Neuro-Native Interface: Beyond efficiency, this methodology inherently provides a "neuro-native" cognitive interface, aligning the AI's internal reasoning patterns closer to human thought structures, which facilitates more intuitive interaction and logical synchronization..
